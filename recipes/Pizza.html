PIZZA

Description

Pizza is a popular dish originating from Italy, consisting of a round, flat base of dough topped with various ingredients and baked in an oven. The dough is typically made from wheat flour, water, yeast, and olive oil, resulting in a crispy yet soft crust.

The toppings can vary widely but often include tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and a variety of meats, vegetables, and herbs. Common toppings include pepperoni, mushrooms, onions, bell peppers, olives, and basil. Some pizzas also feature specialty ingredients like prosciutto, arugula, truffle oil, or seafood.

Pizza is known for its versatility and can be adapted to suit different tastes and dietary preferences. It can be served as a whole pie or sliced into individual portions, making it suitable for sharing. Pizza is enjoyed worldwide and has become a staple in many cultures, with numerous regional variations and styles.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 2 Â½ cups bread flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C), and lightly grease a pizza pan.
  2. Place warm water in a bowl; add yeast and sugar. Mix and let stand until creamy, about 10 minutes.
  3. Add flour, oil, and salt to the yeast mixture; beat until smooth. You can do this by hand or use a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook to make it easier.
  4. Let rest for 5 minutes.
  5. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured surface and pat or roll into a 12-inch circle.
  6. Transfer to the prepared pizza pan.
  7. Spread crust with sauce and toppings of your choice.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, 15 to 20 minutes. Remove from the oven and let cool for 5 minutes before serving.
